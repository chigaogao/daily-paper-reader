## Abstract 

Flow matching has recently emerged as a principled framework for learning continuous-time transport maps, enabling efficient deterministic generation without relying on stochastic diffusion processes. While generative modeling has shown promise for medical image segmentation, particularly in capturing uncertainty and complex anatomical variability, existing approaches are predominantly built upon diffusion models, which incur substantial computational overhead due to iterative sampling and are often constrained by UNet-based parameterizations. In this work, we introduce MedFlowSeg , a conditional flow matching framework that formulates medical image segmentation as learning a time-dependent vector field that trans-ports a simple prior distribution to the target segmentation distribution. This formu-lation enables one-step deterministic inference while preserving the expressiveness of generative modeling. We further develop a dual-conditioning mechanism to incorporate structured priors into the learned flow. Specifically, we propose a 

Dual-Branch Spatial Attention (DB-SA) module that injects multi-scale structural information into the flow field, and a Frequency-Aware Attention (FA-Attention) module that models cross-domain interactions between spatial and spectral repre-sentations via discrepancy-aware fusion and time-dependent modulation. Together, these components provide an effective parameterization of conditional flows that capture both global anatomical structure and fine-grained boundary details. We provide extensive empirical validation across multiple medical imaging modali-ties, demonstrating that MedFlowSeg achieves state-of-the-art performance while significantly reducing computational cost compared to diffusion-based methods. Our results highlight the potential of flow matching as a theoretically grounded and computationally efficient alternative for generative medical image segmentation. 

The code is available at https://github.com/yyxl123/MedFlowSeg .

## 1 Introduction 

Medical image segmentation aims to partition medical images into meaningful anatomical regions, which plays a crucial role in many clinical applications such as diagnosis, treatment planning, and image-guided interventions. In recent years, deep learning-based approaches have achieved remarkable success in this field, including classical convolutional neural networks (CNNs) such as U-Net and its variants [ 35 , 24 ], as well as transformer-based architectures that enhance global contextual modeling [ 7, 32 , 16 ]. More recently, generative models, particularly diffusion probabilistic models (DPMs) [ 14 ], have shown strong potential for medical image segmentation by modeling structural uncertainty and producing diverse predictions [33, 34].

Despite their success, diffusion-based segmentation methods suffer from several limitations. First, they rely on iterative denoising processes with a fixed number of sampling steps, resulting in high computational cost and limited flexibility during inference. Second, these methods often struggle to effectively integrate conditional features with the denoising process, leading to suboptimal alignment between noisy intermediate representations and clean semantic priors. As a result, their performance is typically sensitive to specific task settings, achieving strong results on certain benchmarks while exhibiting limited generalization across different modalities and anatomical structures. Although recent works attempt to improve conditional integration, they still lack a unified and effective mechanism to address this issue. Motivated by these challenges, we explore an alternative generative paradigm for medical image segmentation based on flow matching. Compared with diffusion models, flow matching directly learns a continuous deterministic velocity field [ 20 , 21 ], enabling more efficient generation. More importantly, the sampling process is inherently flexible and can be adaptively adjusted according to task requirements, allowing a better trade-off between efficiency and accuracy. Recent studies [ 4]have shown that adopting UNet-style backbones within the flow matching framework can already achieve competitive performance in medical image segmentation. However, existing flow-based methods still struggle to effectively leverage conditional information, particularly when aligning noisy intermediate representations with clean anatomical priors, and are often tailored to specific tasks [4, 23], limiting their generalization across diverse modalities and segmentation scenarios. To address these issues, we propose a novel flow matching based medical image segmentation framework, called MedFlowSeg . The key idea is to introduce a dual-conditioning architecture to bridge the gap between noisy flow states and clean semantic representations. Specifically, we design a Dual-Branch Spatial Attention (DB-SA) mechanism to inject multi-frequency structural priors into the flow network, providing stable guidance on both coarse anatomical layouts and fine boundary details. In addition, we introduce a Frequency-Aware Attention (FA-Attention) module to model cross-domain interactions in both spatial and frequency domains, enabling effective semantic alignment between the two streams through discrepancy-aware fusion and time-conditioned modulation. Our main contributions can be summarized as: (1) We are the first to propose a unified flow matching based framework for general medical image segmentation, enabling consistent modeling across diverse tasks and modalities. (2) We design a dual-conditioning architecture, including a Dual-Branch Spatial Attention (DB-SA) module for multi-frequency structural guidance and a Frequency-Aware Attention (FA-Attention) mechanism for discrepancy-aware semantic alignment across spatial and spectral domains. (3) Extensive experiments on multiple benchmarks across different modalities demonstrate that MedFlowSeg consistently achieves state-of-the-art performance over CNN-based, transformer-based, diffusion-based, and flow-based methods. 

## 2 Related Work 

Medical Image Segmentation with CNNs and Transformers U-Net and its variants have long been dominant architectures for medical image segmentation, owing to their effective encoder–decoder design and strong ability to preserve spatial details [ 24 ]. With the development of vision transformers, recent studies have incorporated self-attention mechanisms into medical segmentation networks to enhance long-range dependency modeling and global contextual representation, such as TransUNet, Swin-UNet, Swin-UNETR, and DS-TransUNet [ 7, 5, 12 , 19 ]. Although these deterministic models can achieve strong performance on specific benchmarks or tasks, their generalization across different datasets and imaging modalities remains limited. Moreover, they often struggle to handle irregular anatomical structures and ambiguous boundaries, as they produce deterministic predictions without explicitly modeling uncertainty, leading to suboptimal delineation in challenging regions. 

Generative Medical Image Segmentation with Diffusion and Flow Matching Recently, generative models have shown promising potential for medical image segmentation by reformulating it as a conditional generation problem. Diffusion-based methods, such as EnsemDiff [ 30 ], MedSegDiff [ 33 ], and MedSegDiff-V2 [ 34 ], leverage stochastic sampling to model uncertainty and improve predictions on ambiguous boundaries, but rely on fixed iterative denoising, limiting inference flexibility. Flow matching provides a more efficient alternative by learning a continuous velocity field for deterministic generation, with recent works such as FlowSDF [ 4 ] and LatentFM [ 23 ] exploring this direction. ## 3 Methodology 

In this section, we present the details of the proposed MedFlowSeg framework. We first briefly review the formulation of conditional flow matching. Then, we provide an overall pipeline of the architecture, highlighting how conditional features are injected into the flow network. Finally, we elaborate on our two core conditioning mechanisms: the Dual-Branch Spatial Attention (DB-SA) for structural condition, and the Frequency-Aware Attention (FA-Attention) for semantic condition. 

3.1 Preliminaries 

We have designed our model based on the conditional flow matching framework [ 20 , 21 ]. Unlike conventional diffusion models that reverse a multi-step noise corruption process, flow matching generative models directly learn a continuous deterministic velocity field. As illustrated by the overall pipeline in Figure 1, MedFlowSeg establishes an end-to-end generation procedure. It defines a continuous trajectory starting from a random noise sample x0 ∼ N (0 , 1) and progressively evolving toward the final segmentation label x1, structurally guided by the raw image condition I. This probability path and its target velocity ut at time step t ∈ [0 , 1] can be formulated as follows:

Within this pipeline, we utilize a neural network parameterized by θ to estimate the velocity. To achieve precise segmentation, conditional features extracted from the raw medical image I are injected into the flow network: 

ˆut = Fθ (xt, I, t ), (2) where the network Fθ takes the intermediate state xt, the condition I, and the time step t as inputs to predict the image-guided velocity ˆut. During training, the predicted velocity ˆut is constrained to match the target velocity ut. 3.2 Overall Architecture 

The overall architecture of MedFlowSeg is shown in Figure 2(a). At each time step t of the flow matching trajectory, the intermediate state xt is fed into a UNet-style Flow Network, while the raw medical image I is processed by a parallel standard UNet, termed the Condition Network. The two branches play different roles: the Flow Network models the time-dependent generation trajectory, whereas the Condition Network extracts purely spatial image priors without temporal conditioning. To bridge the two streams, we introduce two complementary conditioning mechanisms, namely a structural condition and a semantic condition. The structural condition is imposed at the shallow stage of the Flow Network through Dual-Branch Spatial Attention (DB-SA). DB-SA takes the final high-resolution decoder feature of the Condition Network as input, which is transformed and injected into the first encoder stage of the Flow Network. By decomposing this structural prior into complementary low-frequency and high-frequency components, DB-SA provides stable local guidance, including coarse anatomical layouts and fine boundary details. The semantic condition is introduced at the bottleneck through Frequency-Aware Attention (FA-Attention). At this stage, deep condition features from the Condition Network are combined with the flow features to facilitate interaction between the clean semantic representations and the noisy intermediate state. By modeling their relationship in a shared representation space, FA-Attention enables effective information exchange between the two streams, thereby providing high-level semantic guidance for the generation process. MedFlowSeg is trained in an end-to-end manner, where the optimization is jointly driven by the continuous flow matching objective and an auxiliary segmentation supervision imposed on the Condition Network. The primary objective, Lvel in Eq. (3) , measures the L1 distance between the predicted velocity ˆut and the constant target velocity ut. To further enhance the structural representation ability of the Condition Network, we attach an auxiliary segmentation head to its final decoder feature. Specifically, the auxiliary loss Laux is defined as a combination of a multi-class Dice loss and a Cross-Entropy loss: 

Laux = Ldice + αLce . (4) 4The overall training objective is then formulated by jointly optimizing the flow matching loss and the auxiliary segmentation loss: 

Ltotal = Lvel + λLaux , (5) where λ and α are hyper-parameters that balance the auxiliary supervision and the cross-entropy term, respectively. In our implementation, we set λ = 0 .1 and α = 10 .

3.4 Frequency-Aware Attention 

The diffusion process operates on progressively corrupted representations, while the Condition Network provides stable semantic features extracted from clean images. This discrepancy introduces a domain gap between the two streams, making direct interaction in a single representation space suboptimal. To address this issue, we introduce a Frequency-Aware Attention (FA-Attention) module that explicitly models feature interaction across two complementary domains, namely the spatial (real) domain and the spectral (frequency) domain. As illustrated in Figure 2(b), the input features f and c0 are processed along two parallel paths. In the frequency domain, both flow and condition features are first transformed via FFT, followed by FiLM modulation, patch embedding, and positional encoding to form token sequences ˆTf and ˆTc.Cross-attention is then applied to enable interaction between the two streams, where ˆTc serves as the query and ˆTf provides key-value pairs, producing refined frequency-aware condition tokens ˆTF . This branch captures global dependencies and aligns semantic representations with the current noisy state. In parallel, the spatial domain branch preserves the original real-valued features and focuses on explicitly modeling their discrepancy. ## 4 Experiments 

4.1 Datasets 

To evaluate our approach, we conduct experiments across five distinct medical image segmentation datasets. The public ACDC dataset [ 3], annotated with three cardiac structures, is utilized to verify multi-class segmentation performance on cine-MRI. Additionally, to demonstrate the model’s robustness across diverse imaging modalities, we employ four other public datasets: the BraTS-2021 dataset [ 2] for brain tumor segmentation in MRI, REFUGE-2 [ 10 ] for optic disc and cup segmentation in fundus images, the GlaS dataset [ 25 ] for gland segmentation in histology, and the CAMUS dataset [18] for echocardiographic segmentation in ultrasound images.

4.4 Ablation Study 

We conducted a comprehensive ablation study to evaluate the contribution of each component in our dual-conditioning framework, with results reported in Table 3. Starting from the baseline model only with the conditional branch, only marginal improvement is observed when introducing standard Spatial Attention (SA), indicating its limited ability to handle noisy flow representations. From the perspective of structural condition, the proposed DB-SA brings a substantial performance gain across all datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of injecting multi-frequency structural priors into the shallow flow features. From the semantic condition side, incorporating FA-Attention further improves performance, suggesting that frequency-domain interaction effectively bridges the discrepancy between noisy flow representations and clean semantic features. Finally, equipping FA-Attention with the Neural Modulator yields the best results consistently, highlighting the importance of dynamic, time-aware modulation in suppressing noise and enhancing reliable semantic information during the generation process. 

## 5 Conclusion and Discussion 

In this paper, we propose MedFlowSeg, a novel generative framework that formulates medical image segmentation as a conditional flow matching problem. To bridge the inherent domain gap between intermediate noisy flow states and clean semantic priors, we introduce a robust two-stream conditioning architecture. Specifically, we design a Dual-Branch Spatial Attention (DB-SA) module for stable local guidance and a Frequency-Aware Attention (FA-Attention) mechanism to dynamically align high-level multi-resolution features within the spectral domain. Comprehensive experiments across five diverse medical imaging datasets demonstrate that MedFlowSeg significantly outperforms existing state-of-the-art models, particularly in preserving complex anatomical boundaries. By shifting the generative paradigm from multi-step diffusion to highly efficient, deterministic flow 9matching, we believe MedFlowSeg provides a highly accurate and scalable solution that will serve as a strong foundation for future generative medical image analysis.
